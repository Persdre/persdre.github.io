# Mind the Gap

Paul Graham's essay [Mind the Gap](http://www.paulgraham.com/gap.html) argues that income variation in modern democracies signals economic health rather than injustice.

## The "Daddy Model" of Wealth

A childhood misconception persists into adulthood: viewing wealth as "something that flows from parents" rather than something created through productive work. This model wrongly treats wealth as fixed.

## Historical Context

Most human history featured wealth accumulation through theftâ€”taxation, raiding, confiscation. Only with the rise of the middle class did wealth creation become the primary path to riches. "Behind every great fortune, there is a crime" was once accurate.

## Technology's Leveling Effect

While technology increases income gaps, it paradoxically reduces other disparities. Modern mass-produced goods provide quality comparable to luxury alternatives.

## Income Variation as Health Indicator

"Increasing variation in income is a sign of health" in modern societies, indicating technological advancement and productive capacity growth.

## My Takeaway

Suppressing income variation through high taxation reduces overall wealth creation. Focus on absolute living standards, not relative equality.
